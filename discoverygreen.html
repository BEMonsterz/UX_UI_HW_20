Discovery Green Website Redesign

The Problem

How might we improve the presence of Discovery Green so that more families in Houston are motivated to attend events, create more unique experiences, and have peace of mind to return to Discovery Green?

Role & Responsbilities

My Responsibility on this project was to help gather user research and conduct one on one interviews. I also help sketch rapid prototypes and help develop the updated website. I also helped my team conduct on-site tests with park visitors to gather data on the website.

User Research


Proto Persona

Meet Sandy Spence; She is a married mother with two kids. She lives in the Houston suburbs and is a teacher. She wants to enjoy her weekends with her kids at her local park. Still, She is reluctant to go because there is an alarming homeless population that wouldn’t make her feel safe to let her kids play around in, and she has trouble finding family-oriented events that wouldn’t cost her much.


Interviews

At the beginning of our research, we started by visiting Discovery Green. While visiting, we had one-on-one interviews with park visitors and conducted User Observations to see how families interacted at the park. Afterward, we conducted surveys and gathered tons of information ranging from the number of kids, ways people discovered Discovery Green, and what activities they do at Discovery Green. We also conducted online surveys to a local mom group in Houston and gathered unique user insights.
