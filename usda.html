USDA Governement Website Redesign

The Problem

How can we improve the navigation and digestible content on the United States Department of Agriculture website?

Role & Responsbilities + Team Size

My Responsibility on this project was to help gather user research and conduct one on one interviews. I also help sketch rapid prototypes and help develop the updated website.

User Research


Proto Persona

Daniel is a 50-year-old farmer who has had trouble growing his nearby bee farm. Frustrated that he can’t find the correct information to maintain his bees, he visits the United States Department of Agriculture to learn more about conserving his bees.


Interviews

We gathered quantitative and qualitative data from multiple one-on-one interviews with beekeepers in the rural areas around Houston. While conducting the interviews, one significant insight I picked was that the search bar was the first navigation piece that everyone visited. I also made the user attempt to find a page located on the site using only the navigation but had to help the user after they started getting frustrated trying to locate the page.


Key Take Aways

  • Everyone’s first instinct was to use the search bar
  • People were getting frustrated when browsing for the specific page
  • Beekeeping/bees means something different to everybody
  • When searching you would be met with a bunch of links that take you off the main page
  • Lots of content to digest which would cause anger, confusion, boredom, and some wanted to quit the website right away
  • Search bar was the most popular choice of browsing however, when tasked to use Navigation bar every interviewee would get confused and need help
