Exodus - Keep Calm, and Break Time!

The Problem

The app we created called Exodus is to help social media users to moderate their usage and break free from endless scrolling. We gathered multiple one-on-one interviews and made an eject button.

Role & Responsbilities + Team Size

My Responsibility on this project was to help gather user research and conduct one on one interviews in a team with four other UX Designers. I also help sketch rapid prototypes and help bring them to high fidelity.

User Research

We gathered quantitative and qualitative data from multiple one-on-one interviews with people who have social media accounts and asked about their usage and time spent.

Ideation

We then ideated with “I like, I wish, and What if” and synthesized them into a feature prioritization matrix to determine the best features to solve that problem. Once we found the first solution, we wanted to create a tool that read content and would police you when you were browsing. We decided that this tool would be a privacy issue, so we iterated more and developed an eject button.

Low fidelity prototypes & iterations

Prototypes

We started with user onboarding, creating an account, data usage analytics, and filters. Based on user testing on our first iteration, we had to explain better the eject button and how to use it. We removed account creation and the dashboard for the second iteration while prioritizing the customized screen. We added a personalized time for our users to decide when they wanted to break their usage for the third iteration. For our fourth iteration, we added accessibility to our users by moving the time options to the top and making the entire screen into a popup with a warning notification to ease the user.

Current High Fidelity Prototype
User Testing + Outcomes

The main iteration we did was add more options for the user to choose how much time they would browse for and how much of a break they have. This change mainly focused on the customized screen.

Conclusion + Future Opportunities

For this case study was the importance of ideas, no matter how big or small, and how a throw-away idea can change the whole scope of the study. We balanced a fine line between simplicity and usability. We discovered that simple isn’t always best, we are not our users, and sometimes the most straightforward ideas are the hardest to implement. For future iterations, we will add things to do during cool-down periods, day and hour customization, detailed usage analysis, and a browser extension for desktop.
